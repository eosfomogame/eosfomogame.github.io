Rules

The game is inspired by the now famous FOMO3D based on the ethereum blockchain. With the high scalability of EOS and no fees, we will show that usability and gaming experience are the best.

REMEMBER: PLAY FOR FUN. DON'T BET TOO MUCH!!!

Earn EOS:

The game allows you to earn EOS by getting dividends*. Buying the keys early will give you an advantage to obtaining dividends. Then you can win a large part of the final pot** if at the end of the timer you are the last one to have bought the keys. But wait, now comes the fun: every time that 100 keys will be bought by the players the timer will be increased by 30 seconds.

*Dividend rules:

If you choose the red team to buy keys:
60% of your sent EOS will be given to the current key holders; and 30% will be kept in the active pot until the countdown becomes 0.

If you choose the blue team to buy keys: 30% key holders, 60% pot

**Active pot distribution:

Each round officially ends when the countdown clock reaches zero. The last player who have purchased a key before the timer reaches zero becomes the winner. The last team selected by the winner before the timer runs out becomes the winning team.

If the red team wins,
60% of the active pot will be given directly to this last lucky player, and 30% of the active pot will be divided among the red team key holders.

If the blue team wins, 30% of the active pot will be given diretly to this last lucky player, and 60% of the active pot will be divided among the blue team key holders.

Referral bonus:

An earlier play will earn 8% of all the EOS put into the game by anyone that joins through his link as referral bonus. If someone tries to cheat by visiting his own referral, he won't get the bonus as his account is not an earlier player yet. If this earlier player hasn't bought any key yet, he won't get the referral bonus either.

Entrance threshold:

Each time you must buy more than 100 keys at least. When the total sold key amount reaches 1 million, the entrance threshold will raise to 1/10000 of the total sold keys going forward. It's designed to improve the bot cost and lower the bot advantage during the final pot competing process.

Frequently asked questions:

Q: Could the team win the active pot by cheating?

A: The game behavior is fully controlled by the contract code and the EOS main network. Nobody could decide who/which team or when this lucky player could win the active pot.

Q: Why do I sometimes see the wrong countdown time?

A: Your browser will show the correct remaining time wherever your timezone is. But the remaining time could go wrong if you encounter a network glitch which could be resolved by refreshing again.
